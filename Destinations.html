Binurong Point

Binurong Point is one of Catanduanes’ best-kept secrets. A breathtaking cliffside destination in Baras that offers panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean. Reaching the viewpoint requires a short hike through rolling hills and grassy trails, but the reward is nothing short of stunning. Imagine dramatic cliffs, crashing waves, and vibrant greenery meeting the sky all in one frame. It’s a perfect escape for nature lovers, hikers, and photographers seeking raw, unspoiled beauty. Binurong Point is more than just a view, it's a peaceful retreat where you can breathe in the ocean breeze and feel completely connected to nature.

Calaguas Islands

Calaguas Island, located off the coast of Camarines Norte, is a slice of tropical paradise known for its powdery white sands, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and tranquil surroundings. Unlike more commercialized beaches, Calaguas offers a raw, untouched beauty perfect for those seeking peace, adventure, or a digital detox. Whether you're camping under the stars, swimming in its pristine waters, or simply lounging by the shore, Calaguas delivers a serene island experience that feels like a true escape. It’s an ideal spot for beach lovers, backpackers, and anyone chasing that perfect island getaway.

Mayon Volcano

Cagsawa Ruins, located in Daraga, Albay, is one of the most iconic historical landmarks in the Bicol Region. What remains of the old Cagsawa Church, its belfry standing solemnly against the backdrop of the majestic Mayon Volcano tells a powerful story of resilience. The church was destroyed by the violent eruption of Mayon in 1814, burying hundreds in lava and ash. Today, the site serves not only as a reminder of nature’s power but also as a symbol of the strength and spirit of the Bicolanos. It’s a must-visit for history lovers, photographers, and anyone wanting a scenic and meaningful travel experience.
